Our manufacturing emissions calculations are based on the GREET (Greenhouse gases, Regulated Emissions, and Energy use in Technologies) model developed by Argonne National Laboratory.

Components:

  • Glider: Vehicle body, chassis, and structural components
  • Battery: Battery pack production for electric and hybrid vehicles
  • Fluids: Coolants, lubricants, and other operational fluids

The model accounts for material extraction, processing, manufacturing, and assembly emissions.

Electricity grid carbon intensity data is sourced from Ember Climate, with adjustments for transmission and distribution losses.

Methodology:

  • Country-specific generation intensity (g CO2/kWh)
  • T&D loss adjustments (typically 5-10%)
  • Plug-adjusted intensity for accurate EV emissions
  • Annual updates to reflect grid decarbonization

Operational emissions are calculated using WLTP (Worldwide Harmonized Light Vehicles Test Procedure) efficiency data and complete fuel lifecycle analysis.

For Electric Vehicles:

  • WLTP energy consumption (kWh/100km)
  • Grid intensity at point of charging

For Combustion Vehicles:

  • WLTP fuel consumption (L/100km)
  • Well-to-wheel fuel emissions
  • Includes extraction, refining, and combustion

Our calculations are validated against official sources and peer-reviewed studies.

Validation Process:

  • Cross-reference with EEA official data
  • Compare with manufacturer-reported values
  • Peer review by climate scientists
  • Continuous updates with latest research

Average Error: <1% compared to official sources
